How to calculate the Z ratio

Measurement method

You will need to:
  • carefully manipulate the CHP operation
  • ensure you have adequate metering of power and steam outputs
  • decide how to carry out the test without disrupting operations or putting plant or personnel at risk
  1. Start with the condensers at the highest load possible 
  2. Ensure boilers supplying the turbine steam are on fixed output (firing set point) to give a constant flow, pressure and temperature at the turbine inlet
  3. Increase the steam extraction or export in small steps, reducing the flow to the condensers, and observe changes in export and power generation

If some or the entire site heat load can initially be provided by a standby boiler plant, which can then automatically be backed off as the extraction is increased, this will enable the test to cover the greatest operating range.

You may otherwise have to vent surplus steam during the test to permit a reasonable range of condenser loading to be covered.

Where there is more than one pass-out pressure you may be able to determine the Z ratio for each pressure by keeping the other pass-out(s) constant.

Otherwise, you can determine the mean isentropic efficiency in a single experiment and work out the individual Z ratios by calculation.

Calculation method

This also involves on-site measurements but avoids the need to alter the turbine operation.

You will need to use steam tables (this may be via software), a steam enthalpy-entropy diagram, and take measurements of:

  • carefully manipulate the CHP operation
  • at least two of the three mass flows (steam in, steam out and condensate out) and the power output
  • the pass-out steam pressure and temperature and condenser vacuum (pressure)
