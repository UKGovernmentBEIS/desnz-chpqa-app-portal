What is the percentage of uncertainty?

This is a percentage that shows how much your final number might be off due to small errors.

For example, if a CHP system produces 1,000 units of energy with a 2% uncertainty, the actual energy could be between 980 and 1020 units.

How to calculate uncertainty
  1. Identify measurement devices
    List all the devices you use for monitoring in your CHP scheme (for example electricity meters, heat meters)
  2. Obtain manufacturer's uncertainty data
    Check the specifications provided by the manufacturers of these devices. They usually give the uncertainty as a percentage.
  3. Calculate uncertainty for each device
    Note the uncertainty percentage for each device as specified by the manufacturer
  4. Express uncertainty
    Ensure that all uncertainties are expressed as percentages to two decimal places
  5. Provide indicative uncertainty if needed
    If you don't have all the details now, provide an estimated uncertainty. This helps us understand if the uncertainty is within best practice.
Example
  • Electricity meter: manufacturer states ±1.50%
  • Heat meter: manufacturer states ±0.80%
