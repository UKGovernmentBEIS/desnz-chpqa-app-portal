Manage your Combined Heat and Power Quality Assurance programme submissions

Responsible Persons (RPs) and consultants can use this service to:

  • create a new CHPQA scheme on behalf of an organisation
  • submit scheme information to apply for or renew a certificate (which gives access to various financial incentives)

Assessors, auditors and certifiers can use this service to manage submissions made by RPs or consultants.

Why apply for certification?

CHPQA certification means that certified CHP plants can access government support through various financial incentives.

These incentives are provided to improve the commercial case for investing in Good Quality CHP, due to its relatively long payback period, environmental benefit and technical complexity.

They are mostly operational in nature, meaning that CHP plants must maintain their certified status annually to continue to access the support.

The main incentives that certified plants can access include:

  • exemption from the main rates of Climate Change Levy and fuel-oil duty
  • exemption from the Carbon Price Support tax
  • exemption from Business Rates of Power Generating Plant and Machinery
  • the Enhanced Capital Allowance

What you'll need

To register your scheme you'll need to provide details and diagrams of equipment and meters used at your site.

Depending on your scheme, you might also need monthly or annual figures for fuel and heat used, and power generated.

Who can register for an account

You can register for an account if you are a Responsible Person for an organisation or a consultant.

A Responsible Person is someone who creates, renews, or self-assesses a scheme on behalf of an organisation.
